Super Plants

How grass can help mitigate the effects of climate change

By Florian · Class of 2027 · Spring 2026

Do you enjoy going to the beach on a nice summer day? I'd be surprised if you answered no. Have you noticed that many beaches follow a slope downward, forming a small dune? You may not think much of it, but these dunes provide enormous amounts of flood protection to wildlife and infrastructure near beaches. Without these, many areas would see low-level flooding due to storms. Dune erosion, which is an effect of these storms, can lead to said flooding, and in extreme cases, cause the beach to vanish. What would a beach without sand look like? Pretty uninteresting, honestly. No room to play or relax. As climate change causes sea levels to rise, protecting dunes becomes even more of a priority. Scientists have now researched the most effective ways to use plants to preserve these dunes.

Coastal managers and people with beachfront property often include plants in their designs in order to create dunes, protecting areas from flooding (Wittebrood et al., 2018). However, the effect of different patterns and densities of plants on dune height and growth rate is not well understood. A 2023 study by Derijckere et al. aims to give clarity on which planting methods would best lead to dune growth.

Six zones, one beach

For this experiment, researchers closed a large portion of a beach in Belgium off to visitors, creating 6 distinct sections within that area. Each area was 20m by 20m wide. The 6 areas were numbered 1–6. The first area had a gridded plant setup, with low plant density. The middle four had a medium plant density, with number 2 clustered, number 3 gridded, number 4 staggered, and number 5 random. Finally, area 6 is a high plant density, gridded.

The researchers created 3 distinct sections with each of these plant configurations for a total of 18 sections, with 6 configurations. The researchers planted marram grass, which, when planted, was about 40cm tall, and dug 15cm deep. If you have been to a beach, you probably have seen these before; they look a lot like straw.

Measuring a dune, grain by grain

As the experiment progressed, researchers needed to accurately measure dune height daily across the whole research area. To do this, they used a state-of-the-art combination of advanced drones, height measurement rods, and GPS positioning systems accurate to the centimeter. This allowed them to map changes very accurately, and resulted in a detailed elevation map of the beach.

The introduction of plants in all cases increased the height of the sand dune. Dune 1, which contained the low-density gridded plants, was the fastest growing, but did not reach nearly as high a peak as other configurations. All the other zones grew from the seaward side towards the landward side. The area with the high-density gridded plants achieved the tallest overall peak.

"Staggered and random plant formations were noted to cause shorter bedforms compared to the gridded version."

It's also important to note that not only did the areas with plants grow dunes during this time, but most areas around them also experienced a decline in sand height, increasing the absolute effect of planting in resisting erosion.

What remains unknown

However, in the conclusion, the authors note that "the comprehensive effects of vegetation on aeolian sediment transport rates and the subsequent dune development are still complex and poorly understood due to multiple factors influencing the flow in and around vegetation, including cover, density, and spatial distribution."

This paper has opened up the question of specifically how this transport works. While we have answered which types of plant configurations create dunes the fastest, and which led to the tallest and most resilient dunes, researchers have emphasized that future studies should focus on why certain plant layouts alter wind flow so effectively. By refining these nature-based planting strategies, coastal managers can better protect beaches from erosion and flooding in a world where sea level slowly starts to rise.
